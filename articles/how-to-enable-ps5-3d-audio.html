How to Optimize Tempest 3D Audio

Technical Tutorial | Updated April 2026

Hardware Manual Note: The Tempest Engine processes audio on the console level. You do not need a proprietary "3D" headset to use this feature; any high-quality stereo headset connected to the DualSense controller via a 3.5mm jack or USB will work.

Understanding Spatial Audio

Traditional surround sound uses fixed channels (like 5.1 or 7.1) to push audio to specific speakers. The PS5 Tempest Engine uses object-based audio, calculating the exact spatial coordinates of a sound source. This allows you to hear rain hitting the ceiling above you or footsteps creeping up stairs behind you.

Step 1: Enabling the Feature for Headsets

  1. Connect your preferred headset to the console or controller.
  2. Go to Settings > Sound > 3D Audio for Headphones.
  3. Toggle Enable 3D Audio for Headphones to the ON position.

Step 2: Calibrating Your Personal HRTF Profile

Everyone's ears process sound differently. Sony provides Head-Related Transfer Function (HRTF) profiles to match your specific hearing.

  1. In the same menu, select Adjust 3D Audio Profile.
  2. You will hear a rushing water sound moving around your head.
  3. Use the controller to select the profile (Type 1 through 5) where the audio sounds closest to your natural ear level. Do not select the profile that sounds the "loudest"—select the one that feels the most accurately positioned in the center of your head.

Step 3: TV Speaker Calibration

If you prefer playing without a headset, you can now utilize 3D audio through your television speakers by using the microphone inside your DualSense controller to measure room acoustics.

  1. Go to Settings > Sound > 3D Audio for TV Speakers and turn it ON.
  2. Select Measure Room Acoustics.
  3. Sit in your primary gaming position, hold the controller at face level, and remain completely silent while the TV plays a series of pinging sounds to map your room's acoustic reflections.
