Best PS5 SSDs: The 2026 "Pro" Standard

Hardware Analysis | Updated May 2026

Expert Advice: The 2TB Shift

As of May 2026, the storage landscape has shifted. With the PS5 Pro priced at $899.99 and featuring a 2TB internal drive, adding a 1TB expansion is no longer a logical upgrade. To justify opening your console, we now recommend a minimum of 4TB to accommodate the 200GB+ file sizes of modern "DirectStorage" titles like GTA VI.

Heatsink Requirement: Every drive in this manual includes a pre-installed, low-profile heatsink. Do not attempt to run these high-bandwidth Gen4 drives without thermal protection in the PS5's cramped expansion bay.

May 2026 Deep-Dive Reviews

1. WD_Black SN850X (4TB/8TB) - The Industry Standard

Even with the drive at 95% capacity, the SN850X maintains its 7,300 MB/s read speed. For the PS5 Pro, the 8TB model is the ultimate "install it and forget it" solution for enthusiasts who refuse to delete their library.

2. Samsung 990 Pro (4TB) - Optimized for Random Access

The 990 Pro remains the king of random read/write speeds, which is critical for open-world asset streaming. In our 2026 testing, it showed the lowest latency during high-intensity PSSR upscaling sessions.

3. Seagate FireCuda 530 (4TB) - The High-Endurance Choice

The FireCuda 530 is rated for extreme endurance (TBW), making it ideal for power users who frequently download and delete massive 2026 titles. Its heatsink is one of the most efficient we've tested for the PS5 expansion slot.

4. Sabrent Rocket 4 Plus-G (4TB) - Built for Gaming Tech

The "G" stands for Gaming. This drive features specialized firmware optimized for "DirectStorage" and heavy asset streaming. It is our top pick for hardware purists who want the smoothest frame pacing.

The T500 offers premium Gen4 speeds (7,100 MB/s) while pulling less power than the competition. This keeps the internal temperatures of your PS5 Pro lower, helping extend the life of your hardware during summer gaming.
