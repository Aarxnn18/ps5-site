Best PS5 SSDs: 2026 Performance Analysis

Expert Review | Updated April 2026

Hardware Manual Warning: Do not attempt to use a PCIe Gen3 drive. While they physically fit, the PS5 Pro will flag them as incompatible during the formatting stage, and you will be unable to launch PS5-native titles.

1. WD_BLACK SN850X (Editor's Choice)

The SN850X remains the benchmark for PS5 storage. In our 2026 stress tests, it consistently hit 7,300 MB/s sequential read speeds, nearly double the internal drive's baseline. What sets this apart is the integrated Game Mode 2.0, which optimizes the drive's thermal throttling to ensure consistent performance during 4K 120Hz gaming sessions.

  • Pros: Superior thermal management, officially licensed, zero-latency loading.
  • Cons: Premium pricing compared to non-heatsink models.

2. Samsung 990 Pro (The Speed King)

If you are looking for raw bandwidth, the 990 Pro is the current champion. With random read/write speeds that outclass almost every other Gen4 drive on the market, it is specifically designed for the massive asset-streaming requirements of modern open-world titles. The Nickel coating on the controller helps manage heat even during 50GB+ file transfers.

  • Pros: Highest random read speeds in class, incredibly high endurance (TBW).
  • Cons: Firmware must be updated via PC for peak efficiency.

Mandatory Technical Specifications

To meet the 2026 "Hardware Manual" standard, your chosen drive must strictly adhere to the following:

  • Interface: PCIe Gen4 x4 M.2 NVMe SSD.
  • Sequential Read Speed: 5,500MB/s is the minimum, but 7,000MB/s is recommended for future-proofing.
  • Form Factor: M.2 (2230, 2242, 2260, 2280, or 22110).
  • Heatsink: Absolutely mandatory. If your drive doesn't come with one, you must purchase a third-party cooler like the Sabrent M.2 NVMe PS5 Heatsink.
