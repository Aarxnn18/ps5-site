How to Fix PlayStation Portal Stutter and Lag

Network Optimization | Updated April 2026

The "Microstutter" Problem

The PS Portal relies on a stable 15Mbps stream. However, even with fast internet, users often experience "microstutter." This is usually caused by network congestion or the PS5 attempting to maintain a 120Hz handshake with a sleeping TV while encoding the remote stream.

The Gold Standard: Remote play stability is 90% dependent on the host connection. If your PS5 isn't hardwired, you will experience dropped frames. See our Connection Guide for high-shielded Ethernet cables that eliminate packet loss.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Hardwire the Host: Connect your PS5 to your router via a Cat6 or Cat7 Ethernet cable. Never stream from a PS5 that is on Wi-Fi.
  2. Unplug the HDMI: A common 2026 technical conflict involves the TV's HDMI handshake causing stutters. Try unplugging the HDMI cable from the PS5 while using the Portal to force the encoder to prioritize the stream.
  3. Force 5GHz/6GHz: In your router settings, ensure your Portal is locked to the 5GHz band. Disable "Smart Connect" which often forces the Portal onto the slower, congested 2.4GHz band.
  4. Disable 120Hz/HDR: Before starting a Portal session, go to Settings > Screen and Video and toggle 120Hz and HDR to OFF. This reduces the processing overhead on the PS5's internal encoder.

Protect your Portal on the go.

Lag isn't the only risk; the Portal's screen is notoriously prone to scratches during travel.
