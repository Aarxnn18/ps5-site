How to Fix PlayStation Portal Stutter and Lag

Network Optimization | Updated April 2026

The "Microstutter" Problem

The PS Portal relies on a stable 15Mbps stream. However, even with fast internet, users often experience "microstutter." This is usually caused by the PS5 trying to output a 60Hz signal to a TV while simultaneously encoding the stream, or network congestion on the 2.4GHz band.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Hardwire the Host: Connect your PS5 to your router via an Ethernet cable. Never stream from a PS5 that is on Wi-Fi.
  2. Unplug the HDMI: A known 2026 bug involves the HDMI handshake causing stutters. Try unplugging the HDMI cable from the back of the PS5 while using the Portal.
  3. Force 5GHz: In your router settings, ensure your Portal is locked to the 5GHz or 6GHz band. Disable "Smart Connect" which merges 2.4GHz and 5GHz.
  4. Match Resolution: Manually set your PS5 output to 1080p in Settings > Screen and Video to reduce the encoding load.
