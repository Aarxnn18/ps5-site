PS5 Internal Fan Upgrade: Nidec vs. Delta vs. NMB

Hardware Modification | Updated April 2026

The "Fan Lottery" Explained

Not all PS5 consoles are built with the same cooling hardware. Sony utilizes three different fan manufacturers. Some fans have 17 blades, while others have 23. If your console has a high-pitched "UFO" whistling sound, it is likely due to the blade frequency of your specific fan model.

Comparison of 2026 Replacement Options

Manufacturer Blade Count Acoustic Profile
NMB (V3) 18 Blades Silent / Premium
Delta 23 Blades Balanced / Moderate
Nidec 17 Blades High-Freq Whistle

Step-by-Step Replacement

  1. Safety First: Ensure the PS5 is unplugged. Remove the plates and the top intake grill using a T8 Security Torx screwdriver.
  2. Expose the Connector: Carefully lift the plastic shroud covering the fan's power terminal.
  3. Disconnect Power: Use tweezers to gently pull the 3-pin fan connector straight up. Do not pull by the wires.
  4. Swap the Unit: Remove the four long screws securing the fan, lift the old unit out, and drop the new "Silent" NMB or Delta fan in its place.
  5. Test: Reconnect the power, replace the grill, and boot the console to ensure the new fan spins up smoothly without obstruction.

⚠️ Warning: This process requires a T8 Security Torx (the one with the hole in the middle). A standard Torx bit will strip the screws. Always keep track of which screw goes in which hole, as they are different lengths!
