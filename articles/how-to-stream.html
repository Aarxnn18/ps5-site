How to Stream Directly From PS5 to Twitch & YouTube

Creator Tutorial | Updated April 2026

The Built-In Broadcast System

You do not need an expensive PC and capture card to start building an audience. The PS5 has a dedicated hardware encoder chip that allows you to stream high-quality 1080p 60fps video directly to major platforms without impacting your game's frame rate.

Step 1: Linking Your Accounts

Before you can go live, you must securely link your console to your broadcasting platform.

  1. Go to Settings > Users and Accounts.
  2. Select Linked Services.
  3. Choose either Twitch or YouTube and follow the on-screen instructions to authorize the connection. (You may need your smartphone handy for two-factor authentication).

Step 2: Configuring Video & Audio Settings

To ensure a professional broadcast, you must optimize your output settings.

  1. Go to Settings > Captures and Broadcasts > Broadcasts.
  2. Under Video Quality, select 1920x1080 at 60fps (assuming your internet upload speed is at least 10Mbps).
  3. Under Audio, ensure your microphone audio is set to broadcast. If you are playing multiplayer, you must also toggle "Include Party Audio" so your viewers can hear your teammates.
  4. Under Camera, you can connect the official PS5 HD Camera and use this menu to adjust the size, placement, and background removal of your facecam overlay.

Step 3: Going Live

  1. Launch the game you intend to stream.
  2. Press the Create Button (located on the top left of the DualSense controller).
  3. Select the Broadcast icon (it looks like a radio tower).
  4. Enter an engaging title for your stream, and hit Go Live.

Managing Chat Integration

Once live, the PS5 will dock your viewer chat to the side of the screen, or you can opt to have it read aloud using the text-to-speech accessibility feature. For the most professional experience, we recommend turning off the on-screen chat overlay and reading chat from a laptop or smartphone to preserve your full-screen gaming experience.
