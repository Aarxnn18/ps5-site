How to Setup Your First PS5 Sim Rig

Hardware Tutorial | Updated April 2026

From Desk to Cockpit

Setting up a racing wheel on a PS5 is more than just plugging it in. To truly shave seconds off your lap times in Gran Turismo 7 or F1 26, you need a stable foundation and proper ergonomics.

1. Mounting: Stability is Speed

If your wheel is clamped to a wobbly desk, you lose the fine details of the force feedback. If you can't afford a full cockpit, look for a dedicated **Wheel Stand**. These provide a rigid frame for both your wheel and pedals, preventing them from sliding away during heavy braking.

2. The Perfect FOV (Field of View)

The most common mistake is sitting too far from the screen. For the most realistic experience, your monitor or TV should be as close to the back of your wheel base as possible. This helps your brain judge cornering speeds and distances more accurately.

3. Pedal Calibration

Most modern pedals (like the T248R or G923) allow you to adjust the spring tension. For racing, you want a stiff brake pedal. This allows you to use **muscle memory** based on pressure, rather than just how far the pedal travels, which is the key to consistent braking.

4. Cable Management

Sim racing gear involves a lot of wires (Power, USB, Pedals, Shifter). Use cable ties to secure them to your rig frame. A loose cable getting caught under your brake pedal is a quick way to end a race!
