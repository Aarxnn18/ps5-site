How to Setup Your First Sim Rig

Hardware Tutorial | Updated April 2026

The Perfect Racing Environment

Buying the wheel is only half the battle. To be consistent on the track, you need a setup that doesn't move and places your body in the correct racing position for hours of comfort.

1. The "Desk Slip" Solution

If you don't have a dedicated cockpit, your pedals will likely slide on carpet or wood. Use a heavy object or a piece of non-slip matting behind your pedal set. For the wheel, ensure your desk is thick enough for the clamps to bite securely without wobbling.

2. Ergonomics & Eye Level

Your screen should be at eye level. If you're looking down or up, you'll suffer from neck strain during long endurance races. Position your pedals so your knees have a slight bend even when the brake is fully depressed to avoid leg fatigue.

3. Managing the Chaos

Racing rigs generate a lot of cables (Power, USB, Pedals, Shifters). Use cable sleeves or velcro ties to bundle your lines together. This prevents you from accidentally tripping over them and ripping the ports out of your PS5 mid-race.

4. FOV and Screen Distance

For the most realistic experience, sit as close to your screen as possible. This narrows your Field of View (FOV) and helps your brain judge speed and braking distances more accurately, leading to faster lap times.
