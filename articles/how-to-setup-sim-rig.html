How to Setup Your First Sim Rig

Hardware Tutorial | Updated May 2026

The Perfect Racing Environment

Buying the wheel is only half the battle. To be consistent on the track, you need a setup that doesn't move and places your body in the correct racing position. On the **PS5 Pro**, high-torque Direct Drive wheels require a more rigid foundation than older belt-driven models to prevent "flex" from dampening the feedback detail.

🏁 The Stability Checklist

Consistency in racing comes from muscle memory, which is impossible if your gear is moving:

  • Pedal Grip: Prevent sliding with a Rubberized Heavy-Duty Floor Mat.
  • Wheel Clamp: For Direct Drive wheels, use Steel Desk Reinforcement Plates to protect your furniture.
  • Chair Lock: If using a wheeled office chair, swap the casters for Bell Glide Office Chair Stoppers so you don't push yourself backward when braking.
  • Cable Health: Bundle high-vibration power lines with Neoprene Cable Sleeves.
Hardware Tip: If you haven't picked your hardware yet, ensure you choose a wheel with "Direct Drive" for 2026 compatibility. See our Best PS5 Racing Wheels for a full breakdown of torque and feedback specs.

1. The "Desk Slip" Solution

If you don't have a dedicated cockpit, your pedals will likely slide on carpet or wood. Use a heavy object or a piece of non-slip matting behind your pedal set. For the wheel, ensure your desk is thick enough for the clamps to bite securely without wobbling. Tip: A simple piece of shelf liner between the clamp and the desk can double your grip strength.

2. Ergonomics & Eye Level

Your screen should be at eye level. If you're looking down or up, you'll suffer from neck strain during long endurance races. Position your pedals so your knees have a slight bend even when the brake is fully depressed to avoid leg fatigue.

⚠️ Driver's Note: At 120Hz, even a millisecond of input lag can cause you to miss an apex. Ensure you are using a Certified 48Gbps HDMI 2.1 cable to keep the frame delivery perfectly synced with your wheel's input.

3. FOV and Screen Distance

For the most realistic experience, sit as close to your screen as possible. This narrows your Field of View (FOV) and helps your brain judge speed and braking distances more accurately. If you find your current screen too small for a close setup, check our Best Monitors guide for ultra-wide options that increase peripheral awareness.

Ready to upgrade from a desk to a real cockpit?

A dedicated frame eliminates all movement, allowing for much harder braking and more precise steering.
