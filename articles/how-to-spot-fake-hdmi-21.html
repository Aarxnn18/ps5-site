How to Spot Fake HDMI 2.1 Cables

Why it matters: A non-certified cable can cause your screen to go black randomly or prevent 120Hz from working at all.

1. Check the Hologram

Official HDMI 2.1 cables (Ultra High Speed) must have a specific holographic label on the box. It should include a QR code and the text "Ultra High Speed HDMI."

2. Use the "HDMI Cable Certification" App

Download the official app from the iOS or Android store. Scan the QR code on the packaging—if it's real, the app will instantly show the manufacturer's name and cable length.

3. Look at the Cable Jacket

A true 2.1 cable will usually have "ULTRA HIGH SPEED HDMI" printed directly on the cable's outer sleeve. If it just says "High Speed" (without the word Ultra), it is likely an older HDMI 2.0 cable.

4. Check the "48Gbps" Claim

HDMI 2.1 requires 48 Gigabits per second. If the box says "18Gbps" or doesn't mention speed at all, it will not support 4K 120Hz on your PS5.
