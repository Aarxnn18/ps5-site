How to Identify Counterfeit HDMI 2.1 Cables

Verification | Updated April 2026

The Risk of "Fake" Cables

Many cables claim "8K" or "HDMI 2.1" compatibility on the box but fail to deliver the 48Gbps bandwidth required for the PS5. This leads to screen flickering, blackouts, or a forced downgrade to 60Hz. Here is how you verify your hardware in 2026.

Performance Check: If you've already enabled 120Hz but your screen keeps going black, your cable is likely a bottleneck. Check our 120Hz Setup Guide to see if your settings match your hardware's actual output.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Scan the Hologram: Download the official "HDMI Cable Certification" app on your smartphone. Genuine "Ultra High Speed" cables must have this QR/hologram code.
  2. Verify the Brand: Use the app to scan the code. It should return the exact brand and model name. If the app shows "Invalid" or a different brand, return the cable immediately.
  3. Check the Jacket: Look for the "Ultra High Speed HDMI" text printed directly on the cable insulation. If it only says "High Speed" or "High Speed with Ethernet," it is an older 18Gbps cable.
  4. Pin Inspection: Inspect the connector. Genuine 2.1 cables have 19 pins. If pins are missing or look crooked, the cable will suffer from signal noise.
