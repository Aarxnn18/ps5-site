How to Identify Counterfeit HDMI 2.1 Cables

Technical Tutorial | Updated April 2026

The Bandwidth Bottleneck: The term "HDMI 2.1" is heavily abused in marketing. Due to lax naming conventions, manufacturers are legally allowed to label cables as 2.1 even if they only support legacy 18Gbps speeds. For the PS5 Pro, you specifically need 48Gbps.

The Symptoms of a Fake Cable

If you have purchased a fraudulent cable, your console will experience severe data bottlenecks. Common symptoms include:

  • The inability to select 4K resolution and 120Hz simultaneously in the PS5 menu.
  • Random 2 to 3-second blackouts where the TV loses the signal during intense gameplay.
  • "Snow" or tiny sparkling white dots appearing across dark areas of the screen.
  • Audio dropping out completely for split seconds.

Step 1: Checking the Packaging

An official, fully capable 48Gbps cable will never simply say "HDMI 2.1." It must be officially certified by the HDMI Forum. Look exclusively for the exact phrase: Ultra High Speed HDMI Cable on the box. If it says "Premium High Speed" or just "High Speed," it is an older generation cable.

Step 2: The Holographic Verification

To combat counterfeits, every single legitimate 48Gbps cable is issued a highly specific holographic sticker.

  1. Locate the sticker on the front or back of the packaging. It will have a silver holographic foil design and a distinct QR code.
  2. Download the official HDMI Cable Certification App from the iOS App Store or Google Play Store.
  3. Open the app and scan the QR code.
  4. The app will immediately communicate with the HDMI Forum database. It will display a massive green checkmark and list the exact brand and length of the cable you are holding. If the app throws an error or the brand doesn't match, the cable is a fake.

Step 3: Checking the Cable Jacket

If you have already thrown the box away, you can still check the cable itself. Genuine cables are required to have the words "Ultra High Speed HDMI Cable" printed directly onto the rubber or braided jacket at regular intervals.
