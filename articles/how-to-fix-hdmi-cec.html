Troubleshooting HDMI-CEC & "One-Touch Play"

Hardware Synchronization | Updated April 2026

The Handshake Problem

HDMI-CEC (Consumer Electronics Control) allows your PS5 to turn on your TV automatically. However, different brands use unique trade names that can cause software conflicts. If your TV switches inputs randomly, it's likely a "handshake" error.

Brand Protocol Map: To fix this, you must find your TV's specific name for CEC.
  • Sony: BRAVIA Sync
  • LG: SimpLink
  • Samsung: Anynet+
  • Vizio: CEC
If your current TV doesn't support these features reliably, see our Best PS5 OLED TVs for perfect 2026 integration.

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. Console Activation: Go to Settings > System > HDMI. Ensure Enable HDMI Device Link is toggled ON.
  2. Power Off Link: Toggle Enable Power Off Link to ON so your TV sleeps when the PS5 shuts down.
  3. The "Cold Boot" Reset: If sync fails, unplug both the TV and PS5 for 60 seconds. This clears the "Handshake Cache" in the HDMI controllers.
  4. Cable Verification: CEC uses Pin 13. If your cable is low-quality or damaged, this signal drops. Check our Verification Guide to see if your cable is a "fake" 2.1.
