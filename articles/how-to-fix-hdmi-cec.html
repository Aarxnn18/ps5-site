Troubleshooting HDMI-CEC & "One-Touch Play"

Hardware Synchronization | Updated May 2026

The Handshake Problem

HDMI-CEC (Consumer Electronics Control) allows your PS5 to turn on your TV automatically. However, different brands use unique trade names that can cause software conflicts. On the **PS5 Pro**, enhanced power states can sometimes confuse older 4K displays, leading to "no signal" errors or random input switching. This is almost always a "handshake" error caused by protocol mismatch.

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. Console Activation: Go to Settings > System > HDMI. Ensure Enable HDMI Device Link is toggled ON.
  2. Power Off Link: Toggle Enable Power Off Link to ON so your TV sleeps when the PS5 shuts down.
  3. The "Cold Boot" Reset: If sync fails, unplug both the TV and PS5 from the wall for 60 seconds. This drains the capacitors and clears the "Handshake Cache" in the HDMI controllers.
    4. ⚠️ Tech Tip: HDMI-CEC data travels specifically via Pin 13. In many "fake" or budget cables, this pin is poorly shielded, causing signal noise that breaks the sync. Verify your cable quality with our Verification Guide.
  4. Cable Verification: Swap to the original cable included with the PS5. If the issue resolves, your previous cable was likely an older 18Gbps (HDMI 2.0) lead.
