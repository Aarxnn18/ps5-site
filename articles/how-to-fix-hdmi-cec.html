Troubleshooting HDMI-CEC & "One-Touch Play"

Hardware Synchronization | Updated April 2026

The Handshake Problem

HDMI-CEC (Consumer Electronics Control) is the technology that allows your PS5 to turn on your TV automatically. On Sony devices, it is called "HDMI Device Link." However, different TV brands use different names (Anynet+, SimpLink, BRAVIA Sync), often leading to software conflicts that cause the TV to switch inputs randomly or refuse to power down.

Step-by-Step Fix

  1. Console Activation: On your PS5, go to Settings > System > HDMI. Ensure Enable HDMI Device Link is toggled ON.
  2. Power Off Link: If your TV stays on when you turn off the PS5, toggle Enable Power Off Link to ON in the same menu.
  3. TV Side Configuration: Navigate to your TV's "External Device Manager." Ensure the brand-specific CEC setting (e.g., LG's SimpLink) is active.
  4. The "Cold Boot" Reset: If sync still fails, unplug both the TV and PS5 from the wall for 60 seconds. This clears the "Handshake Cache" stored in the HDMI controllers.
  5. Cable Verification: Ensure you are using a certified 48Gbps Ultra High Speed cable. Low-quality cables often drop the CEC signal pin.
