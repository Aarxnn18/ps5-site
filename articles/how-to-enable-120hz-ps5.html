How to Enable 120Hz Output on PS5

Optimization | Updated April 2026

Expert Guidance

To use 120Hz, your display must support high-frame-rate output via HDMI 2.1 (for 4K) or HDMI 2.0 (for 1080p). Note that enabling 120Hz often triggers "Performance Mode," which may slightly lower internal resolution to maintain a buttery-smooth 120fps.

Hardware Requirement: If the 120Hz option is greyed out, your display or cable likely lacks the necessary bandwidth. Check our Best PS5 Monitors or Top OLED TVs to ensure your screen is compatible.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Main Menu: Navigate to the Settings cog icon at the top right.
  2. Video Output: Go to Screen and Video > Video Output.
  3. Toggle 120Hz: Scroll to Enable 120Hz Output and set it to Automatic.
  4. Game Presets: Return to Settings > Saved Data and Game/App Settings.
  5. Performance Mode: Select Game Presets and set your console to Performance Mode to prioritize frame rate globally.

Still stuck at 60Hz?

You may be using an older HDMI cable that cannot handle high-speed data.
