How to Enable 120Hz on PS5: The Complete Guide

Technical Tutorial | Updated April 2026

Hardware Manual Prerequisite: You must be using a TV or monitor with a native 120Hz panel and an HDMI 2.1 port. A standard 60Hz display cannot be "overclocked" via the PS5 menu to achieve these frame rates.

The Importance of 120 FPS

Playing games at 120 frames per second cuts your input latency in half compared to 60fps. This means your controller inputs translate to on-screen actions significantly faster, providing a massive advantage in competitive shooters like Apex Legends and Overwatch 2.

Step 1: Console Video Output Settings

First, we must tell the PS5 to allow high-framerate signals to pass through to your display.

  1. From the PS5 Home Screen, navigate to the gear icon at the top right to open Settings.
  2. Scroll down and select Screen and Video, then select Video Output.
  3. Scroll to Enable 120Hz Output and change the setting from "Off" to "Automatic".
  4. Optional: While in this menu, ensure VRR (Variable Refresh Rate) is also set to "Automatic" to prevent screen tearing.

Step 2: Game Preset Configuration

Many games will default to "Resolution Mode" (4K at 30fps or 60fps) to prioritize graphics. You can force the PS5 to always prioritize frame rate.

  1. Go back to the main Settings menu.
  2. Select Saved Data and Game/App Settings.
  3. Navigate to Game Presets.
  4. Under "Performance Mode or Resolution Mode," select Performance Mode.

Step 3: In-Game Toggles

Even with the system settings configured, some modern titles require you to manually toggle the 120Hz option within the game's specific menu. For example, in Call of Duty, you must navigate to the game's internal graphics settings and switch the "120Hz Refresh Rate" toggle to ON.
