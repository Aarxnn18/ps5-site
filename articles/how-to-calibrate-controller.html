Calibrating Analog Sticks & Deadzones

Precision Tuning | Updated April 2026

Combatting "Input Variance"

Even without hardware drift, every DualSense controller has a slight variance in its "Center" point. When adding high-rise thumbsticks, this variance is magnified. Proper calibration ensures that your sticks respond to the slightest touch without drifting when idle.

Performance Tip: Software calibration can only do so much if your physical stick caps are worn down. For better grip and increased range of motion, see our Best Performance Thumbsticks guide.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Physical Cleanse: Use a cotton swab lightly soaked in 90% Isopropyl Alcohol. Rotate the stick and clean the "ball" base to remove skin oils that cause friction.
  2. Access Calibration: On PS5, go to Settings > Accessibility > Controls > Adjust Controller Settings to view raw input data.
  3. Browser Calibration: For deep tuning, connect your controller to a PC via USB and visit dualshock-tools.github.io. This 2026 tool allows you to see your "Stick Center" in real-time.
  4. Deadzone Setting: If your stick has a 1-2% variance, go into your game's "Advanced Controls" and set your Inner Deadzone to 0.03 or 3% to negate the movement.
  5. Hardware Reset: If software tuning fails, use a pin to hold the reset button on the back of the controller for 5 seconds to clear the onboard memory.

Is your stick drift beyond a software fix?

You may need to open the controller to clean the potentiometer sensors directly.
