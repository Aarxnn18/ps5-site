Best PS5 Monitors: 4K 120Hz & OLED Displays

Hardware Analysis | Updated April 2026

Hardware Manual Warning: Do not purchase a 1440p monitor unless you are on a strict budget. While the PS5 now natively supports 1440p, to unlock the true potential of the hardware, a native 4K display with HDMI 2.1 is mandatory.

1. Sony INZONE M9 (The Official Ecosystem Pick)

Sony’s own INZONE M9 remains one of the few monitors perfectly tuned for the PS5 out of the box. Featuring Auto HDR Tone Mapping, the console automatically recognizes the display and calibrates your high dynamic range settings perfectly. The Full Array Local Dimming (FALD) ensures that blacks look dark and immersive rather than washed-out grey.

  • Pros: Perfect PS5 integration, excellent HDR600 brightness, built-in KVM switch.
  • Cons: The tripod stand takes up an awkward amount of desk space.

2. LG UltraGear OLED 27" (The Competitive Standard)

If you play competitive shooters like Apex Legends or Call of Duty, OLED is the only way forward. OLED pixels emit their own light, resulting in a near-instantaneous 0.03ms response time. This completely eliminates "ghosting" (blurring behind fast-moving objects), giving you maximum visual clarity during high-speed movement.

  • Pros: Infinite contrast ratio, true blacks, unmatched motion clarity.
  • Cons: OLED panels are not as bright as Mini-LED in sunlit rooms.

Mandatory Technical Specifications for 2026

To avoid bottlenecking your console, ensure your next monitor meets these strict requirements:

  • HDMI 2.1 Ports: Required to carry the 48Gbps signal needed for 4K resolution at 120 frames per second.
  • VRR (Variable Refresh Rate): Syncs the monitor's refresh rate to the console's frame output, eliminating screen tearing and stutter during heavy graphical loads.
  • ALLM (Auto Low Latency Mode): Automatically switches the monitor into "Game Mode" the second you boot up the PS5, bypassing unnecessary image processing to minimize input lag.
