PS5 Pro: The 2026 "Must-Change" Settings

System Optimization | Updated April 2026

Unlocking PSSR and AI Upscaling

With the April 2026 system updates, the PS5 Pro now features "Enhanced PSSR" (PlayStation Spectral Super Resolution). If left on default, the console often prioritizes resolution over frame rate stability. To truly see the benefits of AI upscaling, your display must be capable of rendering the Pro's increased peak brightness.

Hardware Tip: Standard LED panels often wash out the fine detail PSSR provides. For the best results, we recommend a 2026-spec panel from our Best OLED TVs for PS5 Pro guide.

Step-by-Step Optimization

  1. Enable PSSR: Go to Settings > Screen and Video > Video Output and ensure "AI Upscaling" is set to Ultra Performance.
  2. Ray Tracing Boost: In the same menu, toggle "Ray Tracing Reconstruction" to On for sharper reflections in 2026 titles.
  3. HDR Calibration: Re-run the Adjust HDR tool. The PS5 Pro features a unique 2026 luminance curve that requires a fresh calibration to avoid "crushed" blacks.

Bandwidth & Compatibility

Unlocking 8K output or 4K at 120Hz requires significantly more data than the base PS5. If you experience "handshake" issues or black screens after changing these settings, your current cable likely lacks the 48Gbps throughput required for the Pro's high-fidelity modes.
