How to Pair, Reset, and Update DualSense Controllers

Technical Tutorial | Updated April 2026

Pairing to Your PS5 Console

Whether you have purchased a new controller or are re-syncing an old one, the initial handshake must be done via a data connection.

  1. Power on your PS5 console using the button on the front of the unit.
  2. Connect the controller to the console using a high-quality USB-C cable. Ensure it is a data cable, not just a charging cable.
  3. Press the PS Button located between the analog sticks.
  4. The light bar will pulse, and your user profile selection screen will appear, confirming a successful sync. You can now unplug the cable.

Bluetooth Pairing for PC and Mobile

The DualSense is an excellent controller for PC gaming and mobile cloud streaming. To enter Bluetooth pairing mode:

  1. Ensure the controller is turned off.
  2. Press and hold the Create Button (top left of the touchpad) and the PS Button simultaneously.
  3. Hold both buttons for roughly 3–5 seconds until the light bar begins rapidly flashing blue in short bursts.
  4. Open the Bluetooth menu on your PC or mobile device and select "DualSense Wireless Controller" to pair.

Performing a Hard Reset

If your controller is experiencing severe stick drift, refusing to charge, or won't connect to your console, a hard factory reset clears the internal firmware cache.

  1. Turn off your PS5 console entirely.
  2. Disconnect the controller from any USB cables.
  3. Locate the small, circular hole on the back of the controller, right next to the Sony logo.
  4. Use a paperclip or a SIM-ejector tool to press the hidden button inside the hole. Hold it down firmly for 5 full seconds.
  5. Reconnect the controller to the PS5 using a USB-C cable and press the PS button to re-establish the connection.
