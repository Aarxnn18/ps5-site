How to Install an M.2 SSD in Your PS5

Before You Start: You will need a #1 Phillips screwdriver and a compatible Gen4 M.2 SSD with a heatsink.

Step 1: Prepare Your Console

Ensure your PS5 is updated to the latest system software. Turn off the console, remove all cables, and let it cool down for a few minutes.

Step 2: Remove the Cover

Place the PS5 on a flat surface with a soft cloth under it. Position the console so the PS logo is facing down and the power button is facing away from you. Gently pull up on the back corner of the cover and slide it toward the base.

Step 3: Open the Expansion Slot

Locate the rectangular metal cover over the expansion slot. Use your screwdriver to remove the screw and take off the cover.

Step 4: Position the Spacer

Remove the screw and the silver spacer inside the slot. Move the spacer to the hole that matches the size of your SSD (usually the hole marked '80').

Step 5: Insert the SSD

Hold the edge of your SSD, align it with the connector, and slide it in firmly at a slight upward angle. Press the SSD down and secure it with the silver screw and spacer you moved in Step 4.

Step 6: Reassemble

Replace the metal expansion slot cover and the PS5 outer cover. Reconnect your cables and turn the console on.

Next Step: When you turn the PS5 on, it will ask you to format the drive. Select 'Format M.2 SSD' to start using your new storage!
