How to Install an M.2 SSD in Your PS5

Hardware Installation Guide | Updated April 2026

Crucial Step: Ensure you touch a grounded metal object before handling your new SSD to discharge any static electricity from your body, which can permanently short the drive's micro-components.

Required Tools & Preparation

You will need a well-lit workspace, a standard #1 Phillips-head screwdriver, and an officially compatible PCIe Gen4 NVMe SSD with a heatsink attached. Ensure your PS5 is completely powered down (not in Rest Mode) and unplug all cables.

Step 1: Removing the Faceplate

  1. Place the console flat on a table, with the PS logo facing downwards and the power button facing away from you.
  2. Grip the back-left corner of the top cover and the front-right corner.
  3. Gently pull the back-left corner UP slightly to unclip it, and simultaneously slide the entire plate towards the bottom of the console. It will slide off with a firm click.

Step 2: Accessing the Expansion Slot

  1. Locate the long, rectangular metal cover near the fan.
  2. Use your Phillips screwdriver to remove the single screw holding the cover in place. Set the screw and the metal cover aside.
  3. Inside the bay, you will see a small screw and a metal spacer ring located at the "110" mark. Remove the screw and move the spacer to the groove that matches the size of your SSD (usually the "80" mark for standard 2280 drives).

Step 3: Seating the Drive

  1. Hold your SSD by the edges, aligning the notch on the drive's gold pins with the ridge in the console's connection port.
  2. Insert the drive at a firm, 45-degree upward angle. Do not force it flat until it is fully seated in the slot.
  3. Once seated, gently press the back end of the SSD down so it rests neatly on top of the metal spacer you moved earlier.
  4. Fasten the drive down using the small screw. Do not over-tighten, as you risk cracking the motherboard.

Step 4: Reassembly and Formatting

Replace the metal expansion slot cover and screw it in. Slide the plastic faceplate back onto the console until it snaps securely into place. Reconnect your power and HDMI cables. When you boot the console, you will immediately be greeted by a formatting screen. Follow the on-screen prompts to format the drive for PS5 use.
