How to Install an M.2 SSD in Your PS5

Hardware Installation | Updated April 2026

Expert Preparation

Before beginning, ensure your PS5 is fully powered down and unplugged. Placing the console on a soft, lint-free cloth will prevent scratches to the white faceplates. You will need a standard #1 Phillips screwdriver for this installation.

Hardware Check: Most PS5 SSDs require a heatsink to prevent thermal throttling during 2026's high-demand titles. If your drive didn't come with one, see our Best PS5 SSDs guide for compatible thermal plates.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Remove the Plate: With the PS logo facing down, gently lift the back corner of the plate and slide it toward the base of the console.
  2. Open the Bay: Use a #1 Phillips screwdriver to remove the screw from the rectangular expansion slot cover.
  3. Set the Spacer: Move the internal screw and spacer to the "80" mark (most SSDs are size 2280). If the screw is tight, a precision driver from our recommended toolkits will prevent stripping.
  4. Insert the Drive: Hold the SSD at a 45-degree angle and slide it firmly into the terminal until it clicks.
  5. Secure and Close: Screw the drive down into the spacer, replace the slot cover, and slide the faceplate back on.

What Happens Next?

Once you turn the PS5 back on, it will automatically detect the drive and ask to format it. This process takes less than 30 seconds, after which you can begin moving games to your new high-speed storage.
