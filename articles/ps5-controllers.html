The Definitive PS5 Controller Review

Input Devices | Updated May 2026

Expert Advice: The TMR Revolution

In May 2026, we are seeing a massive shift in elite gaming. While **Hall Effect** sensors were the gold standard last year, the arrival of **TMR (Tunnel Magnetoresistance)** sensors has changed the game. TMR offers higher precision and significantly lower power draw, ensuring your controller stays accurate and charged longer—essential for the $899 PS5 Pro ecosystem.

🎮 Pro-Level Selection Guide

Choosing the right input hardware depends on your primary genre and technical needs:

  • Longevity: Look for Hall Effect Sensors to permanently eliminate stick drift.
  • Battery Life: Prioritize TMR Sensors if you play wirelessly for sessions longer than 6 hours.
  • Competitive Edge: Ensure your controller has Mechanical Face Buttons for faster actuation in shooters.
  • Customization: Select Modular Chassis designs to replace sticks without opening the casing.

1. DualSense Edge - The Native Choice

Despite being older tech, the Edge remains king for the PS5 Pro due to its native software integration. While it uses standard sensors, the ability to swap stick modules for $19 remains a unique safety net against wear and tear.

2. Razer Raiju V3 Pro - The TMR Powerhouse

This is the first major PS5 controller to feature Symmetrical TMR Thumbsticks. This tech translates subtle movements into ultra-accurate in-game actions. Combined with 4 removable mouse-click back buttons, it is the most advanced shooter controller on the market today.

⚠️ Tech Tip: Using third-party controllers often disables native PS5 haptic feedback. For single-player immersion, we recommend sticking to the DualSense Edge or official modules.

3. Victrix Pro BFG (Reloaded) - The Modular King

The Pro BFG is the choice for tournament players. It allows you to swap the entire layout, including a specialized module for fighting games. The 2026 "Reloaded" iteration now features Hall Effect sticks as standard.

4. HexGaming Phantom - The Pro Custom

Built on the original DualSense motherboard, the Phantom keeps the haptics and adaptive triggers that other third-parties lose. It adds 4 remappable back buttons and Hall Effect sticks, offering the perfect blend of official feel and pro performance.

5. Nacon Revolution 5 Pro - Asymmetric Durability

For players who prefer the Xbox-style offset stick layout, the Nacon is the only officially licensed option. It uses magnetic Hall Effect tech in both the sticks and the triggers to eliminate mechanical wear entirely.
