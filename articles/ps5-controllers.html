The Definitive PS5 Controller Review

Hardware Comparison | April 2026

The Evolution of Input: Hall Effect Sensors

In 2026, the biggest shift in controller technology is the move away from traditional potentiometers to Hall Effect sensors. These use magnets to track movement, meaning there are no physical parts to wear down—effectively eliminating stick drift forever.

1. DualSense Edge (The Official Pro Option)

Sony’s first-party pro controller is still the only one that offers deep system-level integration. The ability to swap out entire analog stick modules for $20 makes it a long-term investment. The haptic feedback and adaptive triggers remain the most immersive in the industry.

  • Key Feature: Replaceable stick modules and custom trigger stops.
  • Real Review: Great for immersion, but the battery life remains its biggest weakness.

2. Victrix Pro BFG (The Competitive Edge)

For those who play fighting games or high-stakes shooters, the Victrix Pro BFG offers a modular design. You can flip the right analog stick and face buttons to create a "Fightpad" layout. It omits haptic feedback to focus entirely on low-latency wireless performance.

  • Key Feature: Modular faceplates and 4 mappable back buttons.
  • Real Review: Incredibly light and fast, but lacks the "premium weight" of the Edge.
