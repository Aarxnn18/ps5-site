Essential Gear: PlayStation Portal Accessories

Handheld Hardware | Updated May 2026

Expert Advice: The Audio & Protection Gap

The PlayStation Portal's 8-inch LCD is its greatest asset, but in 2026, owners must solve two specific hardware hurdles: the lack of standard Bluetooth audio and the device's unique ergonomics. Unlike a standard controller, the Portal’s weight distribution makes drops particularly dangerous for the screen and analog sticks. These picks focus on PlayStation Link compatibility and high-impact travel protection.

1. PowerA Protection Case - The Secure Standard

Built with a rigid outer shell and a soft-touch interior, the PowerA case is the premium choice for safeguarding your handheld. It features a custom-molded interior that prevents the analog sticks from being compressed during travel and includes a sturdy handle for easy transport between sessions.

2. Fenolical 9H Tempered Glass Protector

Protecting that massive 8-inch screen is mandatory. Fenolical’s tempered glass offers high-definition clarity and 9H hardness, ensuring the Portal's display remains immune to scratches and impacts. Its oleophobic coating also helps minimize the fingerprints common with touch-heavy handheld gaming.

3. Skull & Co. Savage Raven Thumbstick Caps

The Portal's sticks are smaller than the standard DualSense, which can lead to fatigue. These caps increase the surface area and provide a high-friction grip, making competitive play and long-range aiming significantly more comfortable for extended sessions.

4. Sony Pulse Explore Wireless Earbuds

The gold standard for Portal audio. Since the Portal lacks Bluetooth, these are the only earbuds that natively connect via PlayStation Link technology. They offer lossless, ultra-low latency audio, ensuring your game sound is perfectly synced with the on-screen action.

The Network Foundation: Ending Remote Play Stutter

No accessory matters more than your home network. Even with a PS5 Pro, the Portal will stutter if your router cannot handle the high-bandwidth 2026 traffic. Upgrading to a Wi-Fi 7 backbone is the only way to ensure a "local-feel" experience.

TP-Link Archer BE800 (Wi-Fi 7)

With 19Gbps speeds and Multi-Link Operation (MLO), the BE800 provides the dedicated lanes necessary to stream 4K assets to your Portal without the micro-stutter typical of older Wi-Fi 6 routers.
