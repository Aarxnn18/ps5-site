PS5 CMOS Battery: Preservation & Replacement

Deep Hardware Repair | Updated May 2026

Why the CMOS Battery Matters

The CR2032 Lithium battery on the motherboard powers the "Real-Time Clock" (RTC). If this battery fails, your PS5 will lose its date and time settings every time it is unplugged. In a "no-internet" scenario, this can prevent digital games from launching due to license verification logic. On the **PS5 Pro**, maintaining this clock is essential for the PSSR AI-scaling timestamps.

🛠️ Teardown Essentials

This is a high-risk repair. Using the wrong tools will strip the internal security screws:

  • Precision: T8 Security Torx & Phillips #00 (See Verified Repair Kits).
  • Safety: Anti-Static Wrist Strap to prevent motherboard short-circuits.
  • Organization: Magnetic Project Mat (Crucial for tracking 40+ screws).
  • Consumables: Fresh CR2032 Lithium Coin Cells (Multipack recommended).

Step-by-Step Replacement

  1. Full Disassembly: You must remove the plates, fan, and main plastic housing. This reveals the inner metal shield that protects the motherboard.
    2. ⚠️ Tech Tip: Once the metal shield is removed, you will expose the Liquid Metal thermal compound. Do not touch or wipe this. If it spills, you will need a Liquid Metal Replacement Kit to avoid overheating.
  2. Locate the Battery: On the PS5 motherboard, the CMOS battery is in a circular socket near the Southbridge chipset.
  3. Release the Cell: Use a non-conductive spudger to gently push the metal tab. The battery will pop upward.
  4. Insert New Hardware: Replace it with a fresh CR2032 3V Lithium cell. Ensure the (+) side faces up.
