PS5 CMOS Battery: Preservation & Replacement

Deep Hardware Repair | 2026 Manual

Why the CMOS Battery Matters

The CR2032 Lithium battery on the motherboard powers the "Real-Time Clock" (RTC). If this battery fails, your PS5 will lose its date and time settings every time it is unplugged. In a "no-internet" scenario, this can prevent digital games from launching due to license verification logic.

Repair Note: This is a "Deep Teardown" repair. You will encounter several screw types, including Security Torx and various Phillips lengths. To avoid stripping the chassis, we recommend using the precision drivers found in our Best PS5 Toolkits guide.

Step-by-Step Replacement

  1. Full Disassembly: This is an advanced repair. You must remove the plates, the fan, and the main plastic housing to access the motherboard shielded plate.
  2. Efficiency Tip: Since you have to remove the fan to get to the CMOS battery, consider checking your fan model. If you have the louder 17-blade version, see our Fan Replacement Guide to swap it while the console is open.
  3. Locate the Battery: On the standard PS5 motherboard, the CMOS battery is housed in a circular plastic socket near the Southbridge chipset.
  4. Release the Cell: Use a non-conductive plastic spudger to gently push the metal tab. The battery will pop upward.
  5. Insert New Hardware: Replace it with a fresh CR2032 3V Lithium cell. Ensure the "plus" (+) side is facing up.

Are you prepared for a full teardown?

Don't start this repair without a magnetic work mat and a T8 Security bit.
