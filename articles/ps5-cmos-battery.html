PS5 CMOS Battery: Preservation & Replacement

Deep Hardware Repair | 2026 Manual

Why the CMOS Battery Matters

The CR2032 Lithium battery on the motherboard powers the "Real-Time Clock" (RTC). If this battery fails, your PS5 will lose its date and time settings every time it is unplugged. In a "no-internet" scenario, this can prevent digital games from launching due to license verification logic.

Step-by-Step Replacement

  1. Full Disassembly: This is an advanced repair. You must remove the plates, the fan, and the main plastic housing to access the motherboard shielded plate.
  2. Locate the Battery: On the standard PS5 motherboard, the CMOS battery is housed in a circular plastic socket near the Southbridge chipset.
  3. Release the Cell: Use a non-conductive plastic spudger to gently push the metal tab. The battery will pop upward.
  4. Insert New Hardware: Replace it with a fresh CR2032 3V Lithium cell. Ensure the "plus" (+) side is facing up.
  5. Re-Sync: Once reassembled, boot the console and navigate to Settings > System > Date and Time to sync via the internet.

⚠️ Warning: Opening the inner metal shielding of the PS5 may void your manufacturer warranty. Only perform this repair if your console is out of warranty and experiencing "Clock Reset" errors.
